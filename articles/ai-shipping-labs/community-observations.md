# Community Observations

Patterns noticed across community members that might be worth acting on. Individual interview notes live in [interviews/](interviews/_index.md) - this file is for observations that cut across people.

## Perfectionism and needing to understand everything

Jakob is not actively going through the course. From what Alexey could tell, Jakob has already taken some other course, and what Alexey has made so far is too basic for him. Alexey told him more modules are coming, so it will be interesting to get Jakob's feedback on those[^1].

Jakob said some courses he has already done, so for him... Alexey does not fully understand what Jakob's problem is. What exactly is unclear, he has not been able to pull out of him so far - specifically where the blockers are[^1].

One interesting thing Jakob said: he wants to understand everything literally. He does something, and until he understands it all, he does not move on. That might be exactly the problem. Jakob is probably not the only person like this - others exist too. If the same pattern shows up across community members, it is worth thinking about what to do about it. Some kind of perfectionism, or, if not perfectionism, then a strong desire to understand everything before moving forward[^1].

Alexey's own philosophy is different. You do not always need to dive deep into the details - it works, that is enough. That is at least his philosophy. So it is interesting how such people can be helped, because it seems they waste time trying to understand everything thoroughly, and that is why they do not progress. He has a suspicion that this is exactly Jakob's problem[^1].

Valeriia can relate to this pattern. The core issue is that when a person tries to understand something deeply, they do not know what level of depth is needed. For example, on one of their earlier calls Alexey and Valeriia discussed that an ML engineer does not need to dive into theory that deeply and know how to manually write gradient descent or anything like that. Valeriia used to think that was required to get hired. When a person first enters a field, they have no understanding of how deeply each concept and framework needs to be understood, or what is required on the job[^2].

Valeriia also thought Jakob would go through the course more actively, because he said he has experience with a previous course he had taken and this one would still be useful to him. Maybe he just has work right now (some days working, some days free) and he has not started the course yet. We will see[^2].

## No clear picture of the role

Valeriia also could not get Jakob to say specifically what is unclear to him. He explained that he knows some historical facts and some theory about computers, but about everything else he said he does not know where he wants to start[^3].

It might be useful for him, including for the perfectionism, to build on the AI Engineer Role research Alexey did. Maybe based on that, put together some kind of plan. Not a plan exactly, but the site already has a Learning Path (maybe adapt that Learning Path for him, or just sketch the general picture: here is what you can aim for, here is where you are now, and here is what you need to know) this and this and this[^3].

The site currently says, for example, "you know Python", and that is a abstract concept. What exactly should you know? Should you know algorithms super well and be able to solve algorithm problems, or something else - write functions, write tests? It is not clear to newcomers at what level each of these skills needs to be held[^3].

## Idea: Lightning Lesson on the depth of skills needed

Another idea: if there are more people like Jakob who do not know anything about the role, or know little, there is a lot of information on the internet saying "you must learn this, you must learn that", but it feels like there is no expert or person who knows the field saying "you should know this at this level, and that one at a different level". The work is about building products, and to build products you need to understand this, this, and this[^4].

Possibly it would be interesting to make a Lightning Lesson for such people. It seems hard for newcomers to understand the depth of skills needed at the initial stage. And then, depending on the company (startup or large company) the skills they want from you will probably also differ. It would also help to have some rough forecast: if you start from zero, roughly how much time you need to spend learning to reach a level where you can work. Of course this is individual, but even an approximate estimate would help[^4].

This might also be useful for people who are not such beginners on the technical side but still know little about the role itself[^5].
