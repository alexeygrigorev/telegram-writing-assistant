# Interview: Koray Can Canut

## Persona

Sam - the technical professional moving to AI. Koray is a data analyst / analytics engineer with an existing AI project (image taking and analysis) who wants to make it more agentic and add RAG, and is exploring a career change to junior data engineer or junior AI engineer. He has coding skills from analytics work but is in the gap between "scripts" and "systems" that defines Sam.

See [personas.md](../personas.md) for full persona definitions.

## Call with Valeriia

## Initial project status and community structure

Koray currently has a project idea, which came from a suggestion from his wife. He considers it similar to the "easy" level projects listed on the community's website, such as taking a picture and analyzing it. He asked how the community guides members (whether it is structured like a course) and asked about availability of materials such as text, video, and code repositories[^1].

Valeriia noted that finding a project idea is often a struggle for new members and confirmed that Koray's existing idea is a cool starting point[^1].

## Community interaction and sprint planning

Valeriia explained that the main value of the community is the interaction between members. The community currently has about 78 people on Slack. Activities are organized around sprints, planned for accountability. The first sprint is scheduled for May 1st. Participants in the sprint can receive a personal plan from Alexey based on their goals, and the sprints include weekly or semi-monthly standups where people share progress and receive feedback[^1].

## Workshops and content development

The community plans building sessions, which include Alexey's workshops and workshops prepared by Valeriia focused on personal brand development and online presence. Valeriia confirmed she handles content creation, including posts for Alexey's LinkedIn and Twitter, and the newsletter[^1].

All live streams and online events are reformatted into written content and documentation so members can review the material and code at their own pace[^1].

## Group learning and premium course content

The community has an idea for group learning, where members research a topic individually and then meet to share their findings and receive feedback[^1].

For premium members, additional short mini-courses are offered, including Python for engineers, guidance on refactoring AI-generated code, and spec-driven development using agent teams. Koray specifically confirmed that the materials on AI refactoring and spec-driven development are part of the premium tier[^1].

## Personalized guidance and resource access

Koray asked how he should proceed once he receives guidance on a project - whether he would need to do his own research or if specific content would be provided[^1].

Valeriia explained that Alexey's previous AI workshops, including the LLM Zoomcamp, will be transformed into documented lessons and stored on the platform, and that Alexey includes specific material recommendations inside the personalized plans. The personalized plans specify action points such as "week one try this, week two try this," and Alexey offers to review work and provide feedback[^1].

## Community interaction rigor and office hours

Koray asked how community interaction is planned to be more rigorous compared to free courses like the Data Engineering Zoomcamp[^1].

Valeriia answered that, unlike the free courses where interactions can become messy, AI Shipping Labs allows Alexey to dedicate more attention through regular office hours (for example once a week) to answer questions and provide more personal feedback[^1].

Koray suggested that running office hours in small groups (e.g. one expert to five people working on similar topics) would be efficient for the expert and beneficial for the participants[^1].

## Goals for personal guidance

Koray's main interests are getting ideas and improving his current AI project - for example, making it more agentic or implementing a Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) system. He also expressed interest in career advice, specifically about a potential career change from data analyst / analytics engineer to junior data engineer or junior AI engineer, and what portfolio or skills would be required[^1].

## Next steps for receiving a personalized plan

Valeriia offered to send a list of questions to Koray to gather more details about his current goals and problems, and suggested this is the preferred format for him to share input. Koray agreed to answer the questions and noted he could add his own topics as well. Valeriia anticipated that if the answers are sent today, Alexey could prepare a plan by tomorrow[^2].

## Membership flexibility and future priorities

Koray confirmed he has a five-month freelance project starting next week and expressed the need for flexible monthly membership, noting that he might drop out and resume the community later. His long-term goal remains becoming more familiar with AI engineering topics and deploying AI products to the cloud. Valeriia affirmed that the membership can accommodate his schedule and that it is fine if he has other priorities[^2].
