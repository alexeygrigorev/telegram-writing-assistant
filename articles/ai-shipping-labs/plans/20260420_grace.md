# Plan: Grace

Internal working document. Share only the `Summary` and `Plan` sections with the member.

## Summary

- Current situation: Stockholm-based iOS engineer with 7 years of experience who wants clarity on how to pivot toward ML/AI without getting trapped in information paralysis.
- Goal for the next 6 weeks: build one end-to-end AI solution and use it to clarify the next career direction.
- Main gap to close: translating strong software-engineering habits into AI-specific project work.
- Weekly time commitment: roughly 10 hours per week, with more time available on weekends.
- Why this plan is the right next step: Grace already has engineering discipline; the right move is to combine the Buildcamp structure with one concrete project from the start.

## Plan

### Focus

- Main focus: use the AI Engineering Buildcamp as the backbone for one end-to-end project.
- Supporting focus: choose the project early so the course material immediately maps to something real.
- Supporting focus: use sync-ups, sprints, and accountability to avoid drifting back into research mode.

### Timeline

Week 1:

- Go through the relevant course material and shortlist 2 to 3 project ideas.
- Pick one project that feels both motivating and realistic by the end of the week.

Week 2:

- Build the first local end-to-end version of the project.
- Keep the first version narrow: one clear workflow that actually works is enough.

Week 3:

- Strengthen the core workflow and remove the biggest rough edges.
- Use the course material only where it directly helps the project move forward.

Week 4:

- Add evaluation, deployment, and one iteration loop based on what breaks or underperforms.

Week 5:

- Finish the core version of the project and close the biggest gaps.
- Prepare the project for presentation: architecture, tradeoffs, and next steps.

Week 6:

- Polish the project, write the README/demo materials, and share the outcome in a sync-up or community check-in.
- Decide what the next build should be: deeper AI engineering, more iOS/AI integration, or a second project.

### Resources

- AI Engineering Buildcamp - provides the step-by-step structure so Grace does not have to invent a learning path alone.
- Community sync-ups - useful for momentum, feedback, and accountability.
- Coding assistants already in her workflow - useful for faster implementation and unblocking.

### Deliverables

- One end-to-end AI project with a clear use case.
- A short explanation of discovery, modeling, evaluation, deployment, and iteration decisions.
- README/demo materials that make the project understandable to other people.

### Accountability

- Use short sprints rather than open-ended study blocks.
- Pair progress updates with an accountability partner or community check-ins.
- Bring blockers to the sync-ups instead of letting them turn into research spirals.

### Next Steps

- [ ] [Grace] Use the Buildcamp schedule as the base cadence and map each week to project work.
- [ ] [Alexey] Review the chosen project scope once Grace narrows it to a shortlist.
