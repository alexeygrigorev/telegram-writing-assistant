# Take-Home Assignments for AI Engineers: Webinar Q&A

After the webinar on take-home assignments for AI engineers, several questions remained. Here are the answers[^1][^2].

## Skills for an AI product manager

> What is your perspective about the concepts and tools that an AI product manager should know?[^2]

For regular product managers, the core skills are the same. What gets added is the ability to quickly build a prototype yourself. Show it to users and show it to the team. These are all my guesses. I recommend talking to people who actually do this[^3].

## Getting hired without a CS education

> Have you seen someone get hired into an AI Engineering role without a computer science education?[^2]

Yes, I have. Both before, during the ML era, and now. Computer science education is not critical, but it helps. It gives a certain signal that a person knows something. But that does not mean there are no other ways to get the same knowledge[^4].

When I was conducting interviews (I am not doing that now, but when I did) I never looked at education. I absolutely did not care. I only looked at how the person performed during the interview.

I do not care what university someone attended. I know that in some countries one university is considered more prestigious than another. I am from a small city, from a small university.

I do not care about a person's education. The only signal I look at is how the person performed during the interview. That is it, nothing else[^4].

## Expectations for an experienced data scientist

> What are the expectations from a 7-8 years experienced data scientist while applying and interviewing for these roles?[^2]

There should not be problems with this. Making requests to OpenAI and doing evaluation - data scientists have evaluations as a superpower. Data scientists have been doing this for 7-8 years. I assume you designed experiments, ran them, observed how models behave, and so on[^5].

This is important in AI engineering. What you are most likely missing is the engineering side (again, my guesses). Your Data Science experience lets you bring expertise specifically in evaluations, even at the same level as other AI engineers[^5].

The expectations will not be different. If I am conducting an interview, I will not make allowances for someone without AI engineering experience. You need to know everything.

But I understand that everyone has strengths and weaker areas. I would highlight that you understand evaluations well - that is your superpower. And for things where you might be lagging, pull them up to at least the minimum level[^5].

## Focus areas for a recent MS Data Science graduate

> As a recent MS Data Science graduate, what should I focus on to enter AI engineering, given my experience with RAG and AI-powered document intelligence systems?[^2]

This is my bias. When I see someone, I immediately think - they might know theory, but how are things with practice? I do not know.

So I want to see signals that show practice is fine. You can show a project where you have tests and everything. For me that becomes a signal that you are good with engineering, not just theory[^7].

## Software engineering background

> Must I have been a software engineer or developer before learning AI Engineering and/or Agentic AI?[^2]

Having been a software engineer absolutely helps. Yesterday you saw me implement that thing using Claude Code - and Claude Code did everything. All I had left was to evaluate the solution from an engineering perspective. I then said what was not right and what needed to be done differently[^8].

The engineering part is easily solved by showing "I have an existing project, do the same thing." Naturally you need to do this several times to make sure it works. There is no rocket science in this[^8].

With engineering it is harder, because you need real experience to understand how to build these systems. You can find a job as an AI engineer. If the search drags on, you may need to step back and try yourself as a software engineer. You will still be moving in the direction of AI engineering, because these are important skills for any engineer[^8].

Right now I would recommend trying to break through into what you want to do. If you want to do AI engineering, I recommend breaking through there. See how circumstances go.

I would not recommend dropping everything and going into Software Engineering right now. Instead, think about why it is not working, what can be improved, and keep trying. In parallel you can interview for Software Engineering positions too[^8].
