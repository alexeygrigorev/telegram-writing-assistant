# Take-Home Assignments for AI Engineers: Webinar Q&A

After the webinar on take-home assignments for AI engineers, several questions remained. Here are the answers[^1][^2].

## What should an AI product manager know?

Question: What is your perspective about the concepts and tools that an AI product manager should know?[^2]

For regular product managers, the core skills are the same. What gets added on top is probably the ability to quickly build a prototype yourself to show the team what exactly you want. Show it to users, show it to the team. But these are all my guesses, I don't know what the expectations are. I recommend talking to people who do this[^3].

## Getting hired without a CS education

Question: Have you seen someone get hired into an AI Engineering role without a computer science education?[^2]

Yes, I have. Both before, during the ML era, and now. Computer science education is not critical, but it helps. It gives a certain signal that a person knows something. But that does not mean there are no other ways to get the same knowledge[^4].

When I was conducting interviews - I am not doing that now, but when I did - I never looked at education. I absolutely did not care. I only looked at how the person performed during the interview. I do not care what university someone attended. I know that in some countries one university is considered more prestigious than another. I am from a small city, from a small university. I do not care about a person's education. The only signal I look at is how the person performed during the interview. That is it, nothing else[^4].

## Expectations for an experienced data scientist

Question: What are the expectations from a 7-8 years experienced data scientist while applying and interviewing for these roles?[^2]

There should not be problems with this. Making requests to OpenAI and doing evaluation - data scientists have evaluations as a superpower. Because data scientists have been doing this for 7-8 years if that is all you were doing. I understand that you designed experiments, ran experiments, observed how models behave, and so on[^5].

This is important in AI engineering, but most likely what you are missing - again, my guesses, I do not know the exact expectations - is that you will need to pull up the engineering side. Practically, even if you are at the same level as other AI engineers, thanks to your experience in Data Science you can bring expertise specifically in evaluations[^5].

The expectations will not be different. If I am conducting an interview, I will not make allowances for the fact that someone was not doing AI engineering before. You need to know everything. But I understand that everyone has strengths and weaker areas. I would focus on: first, highlight that you understand evaluations well, that it is your superpower. And for things where you might be lagging, pull them up to at least the minimum level[^5].

## Focus areas for a recent MS Data Science graduate

Question: As a recent MS Data Science graduate, what should I focus on to enter AI engineering, given my experience with RAG and AI-powered document intelligence systems?[^2]

This is my bias, but when I see someone I immediately think - they might know theory, but how are things with practice? I do not know. So I want to see signals that show practice is fine. That you can show some project where you have tests and everything. That is what I want to see. Then yes, for me it will be a signal that you are good with engineering - not just theory, but practice is on par too[^7].

## Do you need a software engineering background?

Question: Must I have been a software engineer or developer before learning AI Engineering and/or Agentic AI?[^2]

Having been a software engineer absolutely helps. Yesterday you saw when I showed how to implement that thing - I used Claude Code and Claude Code did everything. All that is left for me is to look at it and from an engineering perspective evaluate the solution and say what is not right, what needs to be done differently[^8].

The engineering part is easily solved by showing "I have an existing project, do the same thing." Naturally you need to do this yourself several times to make sure it works, but there is no rocket science in this[^8].

With engineering it is harder, because you need real experience to understand how to build these systems. You can find a job as an AI engineer, but understanding that if there are problems with job search, maybe you will need to take a step back and try yourself as a software engineer. But at the same time you will still be moving in the direction of AI engineering, because these are important skills for any engineer[^8].

Right now I would recommend trying to break through into what you want to do. If you want to do AI engineering, I recommend breaking through there. See how circumstances go. I would not recommend dropping everything and going into Software Engineering right now. Instead, think about why it is not working, what can be improved, keep trying. And in parallel you can interview for Software Engineering positions, why not[^8].
