I’m currently working on a project to transform a custom GPT-based knowledge assistant into a scalable paid SaaS product.

Today, we already have a working GPT inside ChatGPT that was built using proprietary educational content from our company, focused on the audiovisual/video production market. The GPT is trained with our internal methodology, documents, and learning materials, and users can interact with it to ask questions and receive guidance related to this methodology.

The current limitation is that users must log in with their own ChatGPT accounts to access the assistant, which creates friction and is not ideal for a commercial product experience.

My goal is to turn this into an independent platform where:
- Users can create accounts and log in directly on our website
- Access is restricted to paying subscribers
- The AI assistant is embedded into the platform experience
- Payments/subscriptions are integrated
- Usage and costs can be controlled sustainably

One of the main challenges I’m trying to solve is designing a pricing and access-control strategy that keeps the business financially sustainable. Since AI usage costs are paid by us through API consumption, I need to think carefully about:
- subscription models
- rate limits
- usage caps
- abuse prevention
- profitability and cost control

At this stage, I’m looking for guidance mainly around:
- overall architecture decisions
- SaaS/platform design
- authentication and subscription flows
- AI cost management strategies
- best practices for scaling this kind of AI-powered product
- tradeoffs between speed of implementation vs long-term maintainability
